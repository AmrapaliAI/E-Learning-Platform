Summary

Artificial Intelligence is a broad field of Computer Science focused on creating intelligent agents that perceive their environment and take actions that maximize their chance of successfully achieving their goals. It encompasses core techniques like Machine Learning and Deep Learning, which enable systems to learn from data, driving innovation in areas like computer vision, NLP, and decision-making.



Introduction AI And Its Core Techniques

Module: Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of Artificial Intelligence that focuses on enabling computers to understand, interpret, generate, and interact with human language. It combines linguistics, computer science, and machine learning to process text and speech data.

1. Introduction to NLP

  • Definition: NLP is the study of computational techniques for analyzing and synthesizing natural language.
  • Goal: Bridge the gap between human communication and computer understanding.
  • Key Components: Syntax (structure), Semantics (meaning), Pragmatics (context).

2. History of NLP

  • 1950s: Alan Turing proposes the "Turing Test" for machine intelligence.
  • 1960s: ELIZA chatbot demonstrates rule-based conversational systems.
  • 1980s-1990s: Statistical methods (Hidden Markov Models, n-grams) dominate NLP research.
  • 2000s: Machine learning approaches (SVMs, decision trees) improve NLP tasks.
  • 2010s: Deep learning revolutionizes NLP with word embeddings and RNNs/LSTMs.
  • 2017-Present: Transformers (BERT, GPT) and Large Language Models (LLMs) reshape NLP applications.

3. Core NLP Techniques

  • Text Preprocessing: Tokenization, stemming, lemmatization, stop-word removal.
  • Text Representation: Bag-of-Words, TF-IDF, Word Embeddings (Word2Vec, GloVe).
  • Language Models: Statistical models, neural networks, transformers.
  • Parsing: Syntax trees, dependency parsing.
  • Named Entity Recognition (NER): Identifying people, places, organizations in text.

4. NLP Workflow

  • Data Collection: Gathering text or speech data from sources like social media, books, or recordings.
  • Preprocessing: Cleaning and normalizing text for analysis.
  • Feature Extraction: Converting text into numerical representations.
  • Model Training: Using ML/DL algorithms to learn language patterns.
  • Evaluation: Metrics like BLEU score, perplexity, accuracy, F1-score.
  • Deployment: Integrating NLP models into applications (chatbots, search engines).

5. Applications of NLP

  • Machine Translation: Google Translate, DeepL.
  • Sentiment Analysis: Understanding opinions in reviews or social media posts.
  • Chatbots & Virtual Assistants: Conversational AI like Siri, Alexa, Copilot.
  • Text Summarization: Automatic generation of concise summaries from long documents.
  • Speech Recognition: Converting spoken language into text.
  • Information Retrieval: Search engines and document indexing.
  • Question Answering: Systems that provide direct answers from text corpora.

6. Modern Advances in NLP

  • Transformers: Attention-based models (BERT, GPT, T5) dominate NLP tasks.
  • Large Language Models (LLMs): Capable of text generation, summarization, and reasoning.
  • Multimodal NLP: Combining text with vision/audio for richer AI systems.
  • Zero-shot & Few-shot Learning: Models generalize to tasks with minimal training data.

7. Challenges in NLP

  • Ambiguity: Words and sentences often have multiple meanings.
  • Context Understanding: Capturing long-range dependencies in text.
  • Bias: Language models may inherit societal biases from training data.
  • Low-Resource Languages: Limited data availability for many world languages.
  • Ethical Concerns: Misinformation, fake content generation, privacy issues.
